Television Energy Consumption in Australia

Televisions are a common appliance in Australian homes and can impact your household’s energy consumption. Choosing an energy-efficient TV helps reduce electricity bills and environmental impact. Look for the Energy Rating Label when buying a new television.

  • Modern LED and OLED TVs use significantly less energy than older plasma or CRT models.
  • Screen size affects energy use: larger TVs generally consume more power.
  • Standby mode can account for up to 10% of a TV’s annual energy use. Turn off TVs at the wall to save energy.
  • Australian households can compare TV models using the Energy Rating Label, which shows star ratings and estimated annual energy consumption (kWh/year).
  • Energy-efficient televisions help reduce greenhouse gas emissions and lower electricity bills.

When shopping for a new television, consider not only the purchase price but also the long-term energy costs. Look for features such as automatic brightness adjustment, energy-saving modes, and low standby power consumption.
